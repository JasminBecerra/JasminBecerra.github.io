Cartographer

This is a long-form infographic about food deserts in Chicago and their correlation to income disparities in a segregated Chicago. I used data from a USDA Access to Affordable and Nutritious Food report to visualize the difference between those who live in food deserts, and those who do not. Halfway through the infographic, you'll find a bivariate map of Race and Income in Chicago cross referenced by food deserts in chicago. Following the bivariate map, you'll find two bar graphs depicting Food Spending data, and finally, a short snippet of how the landscape of food deserts might slowly begin to change.
