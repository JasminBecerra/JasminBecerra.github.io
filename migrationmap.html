Jasmin Becerra

Cartographer

The following collection of photographs are of an embroidered map of my family's migration from Mexico to the United States starting the 1970s.

The photograph above captures my embroidered map in its entirety. For reference, the green routes represent my father's migration and the magenta routes represent my mother's.

The photo above is a close up of the legend on the lower right hand corner of my map. I used dashed lines to represent travel, less permanent movement, whereas the closed routes represent more permanent migration.

This photo is a a close up of the Mexico/Gulf of Mexico region of my map.

This final photo is a close up of the beginning of my parent's migration. Both of my parents are from a small rural town just outside the city of San Luis Potosi. My mother moved to the city of Aguascalientes as an adolescent, but spent her summers (farming season!) in San Luis Potosi. As a teen, my mother traveled with my grandmother to El Paso and Los Angeles to visit her older siblings who had immigrated to the US. When my father turned 18, he set out to chase the American dream and settled in Houston for 2 years before making it to New York City in 1980. He lived in New York for a year before moving to Chicago to live with my great-aunt. My parents were close childhood friends, and as such, they corresponded via mail when my father left Mexico. In 1988 my father returned to Mexico, and married my mother. My mother relocated to Chicago with my father, where the two have lived since 1988. And that is the story of how my family got to call Chicago "home"!
